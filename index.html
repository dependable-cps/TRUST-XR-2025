About the Workshop

Extended Reality (XR) technologies increasingly integrate into everyday life such as education, healthcare, defense, workforce training, and entertainment, the need for trustworthy, secure, and privacy-aware artificial intelligence (AI) becomes critical. XR systems capture sensitive biometric and behavioral data, use opaque AI models, and are vulnerable to adversarial manipulation.

TRUST-XR 2025 will explore cross-disciplinary challenges in securing XR systems, improving AI explainability, protecting immersive user data, and developing fair and ethical XR interfaces powered by conventional and generative AI (e.g., large language models (LLMs)). The workshop will foster discussion and innovation around privacy-preserving pipelines, adversarial resilience, and human-centered trust frameworks.

Trust XR Workshop is for you, if you are:

  1. XR and Immersive Systems Researchers: Investigating the role of trust, security, privacy, and ethics in extended reality environments using biometric and multimodal sensing data.
  2. AI and Machine Learning Experts: Developing explainable, privacy-preserving, and adversarially robust AI models for XR applications, including those powered by LLMs and generative AI.
  3. Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Cognitive Security Scholars: Studying user trust, manipulation risks, and perception-aware attacks in AI-driven immersive experiences.
  4. Cybersecurity and Privacy Professionals: Focusing on real-time XR system vulnerabilities, privacy leakage, multimodal sensor protection, and runtime-layer defense strategies.
  5. Ethics, Policy, and Responsible AI Advocates: Addressing fairness, accountability, and regulatory compliance in intelligent XR systems, including responsible deployment of black-box AI models.
  6. Developers and System Architects: Building secure XR platforms and pipelines that balance immersive performance with safety, explainability, and user trust.

Program Overview: TRUST-XR 2025 will be a full-day hybrid workshop featuring a blend of expert talks, research presentations, and hands-on collaborative activities. The agenda includes:

  1. Opening Keynote addressing the future of trustworthy AI in immersive XR systems
  2. Thematic Sessions with peer-reviewed paper and lightning talks
  3. Interactive Group Activities where attendees will collaboratively assess risks and brainstorm solutions related to:
    • Explainability and robustness in AI-powered XR
    • Secure and ethical handling of immersive biometric data
    • Generative AI and LLM integration in real-time XR content
    • Privacy, manipulation, and trust frameworks in XR environments

Topics of Interest

The TRUST-XR 2025 workshop welcomes contributions related to trustworthy, secure, and privacy-aware AI in XR systems. Topics include, but are not limited to:
  • Explainable and interpretable AI models for XR environments
  • Privacy-preserving machine learning techniques for multimodal XR data (e.g., eye-tracking, biometrics, physiological signals)
  • Adversarial robustness in AI-driven XR pipelines
  • Cognitive security threats and perceptual manipulation in immersive systems
  • Ethical frameworks and responsible AI design for XR applications
  • Security and privacy challenges in generative AI-powered XR content and interaction
  • User trust, transparency, and accountability in intelligent XR systems
  • Secure and resilient multimodal data pipelines (visual, audio, haptic, biometric)
  • Runtime-layer vulnerabilities and defenses in OpenXR-based applications
  • Cross-platform security and privacy issues in consumer and enterprise XR devices
  • Human-centered evaluation frameworks for trust, privacy, and explainability
  • Case studies of trustworthy AI deployments in real-world XR scenarios
  • Designing XR systems that balance immersion with safety and ethical use
  • Bias detection and mitigation in AI models used in XR interactions
  • Security implications of integrating LLMs into XR systems
  • Policy and regulatory perspectives on privacy and safety in immersive computing

Submission Guidelines

We invite submissions that explore the intersection of AI, XR, privacy, and security. Submissions must follow the IEEE VGTC formatting guidelines and will be peer-reviewed based on relevance, originality, and contribution diversity. Accepted submissions will be published in the ISMAR 2025 Adjunct Proceedings and archived in IEEE Xplore.

We accept the following types of submissions:

  1. Short Papers (up to 6 pages, including references): Reporting novel research, findings, or system designs related to trustworthy AI and XR.
  2. Position Papers (2–3 pages, including references): Presenting early ideas, emerging challenges, or perspectives on XR security, privacy, and ethics.
  3. Demo Proposals (2 pages, including references): Describing interactive demos of XR systems, privacy-preserving tools, or AI explainability frameworks.
  4. 5-Minute Video Response: Accompanying any submission type, this video should address:
    • A key trust, privacy, or security challenge in future AI-powered XR ecosystems
    • The AIXR application context
    • 1–2 mitigation strategies
    • Trade-offs, usability, or ethical implications

Formatting

All submissions should use the IEEE VGTC formatting template and be submitted in PDF format. Submissions must be original, unpublished, and not currently under review elsewhere. Papers that fail to comply with formatting or length limits will be desk-rejected.

Review Process

Submissions will be single-blind and reviewed by the workshop organizers and program committee. Accepted papers will be assigned oral or lightning talk presentations based on content and format.
